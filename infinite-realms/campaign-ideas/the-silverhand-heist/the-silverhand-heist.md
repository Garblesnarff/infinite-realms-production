# The Silverhand Heist: The Vault of Eternity

*A Criminal Enterprise Campaign — Medium Campaign (7-9 sessions) — Medium difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** The Silverhand Heist: The Vault of Eternity
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Criminal Enterprise / Heist / Moral Ambiguity
**Estimated Length:** Medium Campaign — 7-9 sessions
**Player Level Range:** Start **3** → Finish **7**
**Tone (keywords):** clever, tense, morally ambiguous, sophisticated, thrilling
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
The players are recruited by a mysterious employer for the ultimate heist: stealing the legendary "Eternity Stone" from the most secure vault in the kingdom, protected by ancient magic, elite guards, and infallible mechanical defenses. As they assemble their crew of specialists and plan the intricate operation, they discover that multiple criminal organizations are after the same prize, each with their own agenda. The heist becomes a web of betrayals, double-crosses, and moral compromises as the players must decide whether to stick to the plan, go for a bigger score, or walk away from the criminal life entirely. But as the stakes rise and alliances shift, they realize the Eternity Stone may be more dangerous than any of them imagined.

---

## Story Arc (Medium Campaign — 7-9 sessions)

A symphony of criminal orchestration.

*   **Act I — The Setup (Sessions 1–2):** The players are recruited and begin assembling their crew while gathering intelligence on the target.
*   **Act II — The Planning (Sessions 3–5):** As the players develop their heist plan, they navigate rival criminal organizations and internal crew tensions.
*   **Act III — The Execution (Sessions 6–9):** The heist unfolds with complications, betrayals, and high-stakes decisions that determine everyone's fate.

---

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Session 1 — "The Job Offer"
*   **Focus:** Introduction / Crew Assembly
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The players are approached by a mysterious employer who offers them the heist of a lifetime.
    *   They begin recruiting specialists: a master infiltrator, a magical safecracker, a disguise expert, and others.
    *   Initial reconnaissance reveals the vault's legendary reputation and the dangers involved.
**Escalation:** The players discover they're not the only crew hired for this job, as rival criminal organizations begin making moves.

### Sessions 2-3 — "The Information Game"
*   **Focus:** Intelligence Gathering / Social Dynamics
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The players gather intelligence through various channels: bribing guards, studying architectural plans, researching magical defenses.
    *   They navigate the criminal underworld, making contacts and learning about competing heist crews.
    *   A breakthrough reveals a weakness in the vault's defenses but also attracts dangerous attention.
**Escalation:** A rival crew attempts to sabotage the players' efforts, forcing them to defend their operation while maintaining secrecy.

### Sessions 4-5 — "The Plan Comes Together"
*   **Focus:** Planning / Crew Management
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The players develop their detailed heist plan, assigning roles and preparing contingencies.
    *   They acquire necessary equipment and forge documents while dealing with internal crew conflicts.
    *   A dry run reveals flaws in the plan that must be addressed before the real attempt.
**Escalation:** The players discover that their employer has been less than honest about the Eternity Stone's true nature and potential dangers.

### Sessions 6-7 — "The Night of the Heist"
*   **Focus:** Execution / Problem-Solving
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The heist begins with the players executing their plan across multiple synchronized operations.
    *   They navigate the vault's defenses: magical wards, mechanical traps, elite guards, and security measures.
    *   Unexpected complications arise, forcing the players to improvise and adapt.
**Escalation:** A rival crew executes their own heist simultaneously, creating chaos and forcing the players to compete directly.

### Sessions 8-9 — "The Double-Cross"
*   **Focus:** Betrayal / Resolution
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The players must deal with betrayals from within their own crew and from their employer.
    *   They decide what to do with the Eternity Stone - keep it, sell it, or use it for a higher purpose.
    *   The campaign concludes with the players' criminal legacy established and their moral choices tested.
**Escalation:** The Eternity Stone's true power is revealed, forcing the players to make impossible choices about its fate and their own futures.

## Major NPCs

1.  **The Shadow Broker** — *The Mysterious Employer*
    *   **Role:** The players' enigmatic employer who brings them together for the heist but has hidden motives.
    *   **Personality:** Suave and calculating, speaking in riddles and half-truths while maintaining an air of mystery.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** They are actually a representative of a powerful noble house seeking to use the Eternity Stone for political gain.

2.  **Lira "Ghost" Voss** — *The Master Infiltrator*
    *   **Role:** A legendary thief who becomes the players' infiltration specialist and moral compass.
    *   **Personality:** Professional and cynical, with a strict code of honor among thieves and a dry wit.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** She has a personal history with the vault's head of security that could complicate the heist.

3.  **Marcus "The Vault" Kane** — *The Security Expert*
    *   **Role:** The head of vault security who becomes an antagonist, representing the "straight" world the players are robbing.
    *   **Personality:** Intelligent and dedicated, viewing the players as the ultimate challenge to his security measures.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** He knows more about the Eternity Stone's true nature than he lets on and may have his own agenda.

## Key Locations

1.  **The Eternity Vault:** The most secure repository in the kingdom, protected by ancient magic and modern ingenuity.
2.  **The Thieves' Quarter:** A shadowy district where criminal organizations operate openly and information flows freely.
3.  **The Noble District:** The wealthy area where the players must maintain their cover and gather intelligence.
4.  **The Black Market:** A hidden network where magical artifacts and criminal services are bought and sold.

## Central Conflict

This campaign explores the thrill of criminal enterprise, the complexity of moral ambiguity, and the consequences of ambition. The central conflict revolves around the heist of the Eternity Stone, complicated by rival criminal organizations, internal betrayals, and the stone's dangerous power. The players must navigate the criminal underworld while questioning whether the biggest score is worth the risk to their souls. The campaign forces difficult questions: When does clever planning become immoral? Can criminals have honor? And what happens when the ultimate power falls into the wrong hands?

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **The Heist Blueprint System**
*   Players create and modify a detailed blueprint of the heist, showing all planned movements and contingencies.
*   The blueprint evolves based on new information and player decisions, creating a living plan.
*   Successful execution of blueprint elements provides bonuses, while failures create complications.

### 2. **The Betrayal Deck**
*   A deck of cards representing potential betrayals from NPCs, rival crews, and even player allies.
*   Players draw from this deck at key moments, creating uncertainty and forcing difficult choices.
*   Some betrayals can be prevented through good roleplaying or clever precautions.

### 3. **The Moral Compass Tracker**
*   Players track their moral alignment through choices that affect their reputation in the criminal underworld.
*   Their moral compass influences NPC reactions, available opportunities, and potential endings.
*   The system creates meaningful consequences for embracing the criminal lifestyle versus seeking redemption.
