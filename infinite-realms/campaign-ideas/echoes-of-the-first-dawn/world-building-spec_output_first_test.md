# The Mythology of the World

## The Pantheon

The world is home to a diverse pantheon of gods, each with their own domains, stories, and influence on the world's inhabitants.

### The Elven Pantheon

- **Corellon Larethian**: The chief deity of the elven pantheon, Corellon is the god of creation, magic, art, and beauty. He is often depicted as a graceful elf with a keen intellect and a love for the arts.
- **Erevan Ilesere**: The trickster god of the elves, Erevan is known for his cunning and mischief. He is the patron of thieves, rogues, and those who enjoy riddles and wordplay.
- **Sehanine Moonbow**: The goddess of the moon, Sehanine is the protector of the elven people and the deity of love, dreams, and illusions. She is often associated with the night sky and the stars.

### The Dwarven Pantheon

- **Moradin**: The chief deity of the dwarven pantheon, Moradin is the god of creation, forging, and mining. He is often depicted as a wise and sturdy dwarf, hammer in hand.
- **Berronar Truesilver**: The goddess of hearth, home, and family, Berronar is the protector of dwarven clans and the deity of loyalty, honesty, and stability.
- **Clangeddin Silverbeard**: The god of war and battle, Clangeddin is the patron of dwarven warriors and the deity of courage, honor, and perseverance.

### The Human Pantheon

- **Pelor**: The chief deity of the human pantheon, Pelor is the god of the sun, healing, and strength. He is often depicted as a radiant human figure, bringing light and warmth to the world.
- **Erathis**: The goddess of civilization, law, and knowledge, Erathis is the patron of human cities and the deity of order, creativity, and invention.
- **Kord**: The god of storms, battle, and sports, Kord is the patron of human athletes and the deity of strength, passion, and competition.

### The Orcish Pantheon

- **Gruum**: The chief deity of the orcish pantheon, Gruum is the god of war, strength, and hunting. He is often depicted as a powerful orcish warrior, wielding a massive weapon.
- **Bahgtru**: The god of brute strength and destruction, Bahgtru is the patron of orcish berserkers and the deity of rage, chaos, and violence.
- **Luthic**: The goddess of fertility, healing, and the wilds, Luthic is the protector of orcish tribes and the deity of nature, growth, and nurturing.

## Creation Myths

The world's creation myths vary between cultures, but they all share a common theme: the gods shaping the world from a void or chaotic state.

### Elven Creation Myth

In the beginning, there was only the chaotic void known as the Elemental Chaos. From this formless expanse, the goddess of magic, Selvaren, and the god of creation, Aureon, emerged. Through their combined powers, they shaped the world and brought forth order and life. They created the first elves, who in turn populated the world with the diverse intelligent species known today.

### Dwarven Creation Myth

Before the world was formed, there existed only the raw materials of creation: the earth, the sea, the sky, and the fire. The great dwarven god Moradin, the creator of all things, took these elements and forged the world. He created the first dwarves from the very essence of the earth, imbuing them with his own strength and resilience.

### Human Creation Myth

In the beginning, there was only the great sea of potential, known as the Astral Sea. From this formless expanse, the gods emerged and shaped the world into a wondrous realm. They breathed life into the void, creating the first humans, who in turn populated the world with the diverse intelligent species known today.

### Orcish Creation Myth

Before the world was formed, there was only the great darkness, known as the Shadowfell. From this formless expanse, the orcish god Gruum emerged, wielding his mighty power to shape the world. He created the first orcs, who in turn populated the world with their kind, establishing their dominance through strength and cunning.

## Legendary Figures

Throughout the ages, the world has been shaped by legendary figures, both mortal and divine, who have left their mark on history.

### Elven Legends

- **Galadriel**: An ancient elven sorceress, Galadriel is known for her wisdom, foresight, and unyielding determination. She played a crucial role in the defense of elven realms during the Age of Empires and is revered as a symbol of elven resilience.
- **Elrond**: A wise and powerful elf-lord, Elrond is the founder of the Council of Elrond and a key figure in the formation of the Last Alliance of Elves and Men. His knowledge of magic and lore is unmatched, and he is often sought after for counsel and guidance.

### Dwarven Legends

- **Thrain**: A legendary dwarven king, Thrain is remembered for his unyielding courage and determination in the face of adversity. He led his people through countless battles, securing their place in the world and establishing their dominance in the realms of mining and forging.
- **Durin**: The first dwarf, Durin is considered the father of the dwarven race. He is said to have lived for thousands of years, guiding his people through the ages and imparting his wisdom upon them.

### Human Legends

- **Alexander**: A mighty human conqueror, Alexander is known for his military prowess and ambition. He forged a vast empire, stretching from the west to the east, and is remembered as a symbol of human ingenuity and determination.
- **Da Vinci**: A brilliant human artist, inventor, and polymath, Da Vinci is revered for his contributions to science, art, and engineering. His works have inspired generations of thinkers and creators, and he is often hailed as a symbol of human curiosity and innovation.

### Orcish Legends

- **Grom Hellscream**: A fierce and cunning orcish warlord, Grom is remembered for his unwavering loyalty to his people and his relentless pursuit of victory. He led his horde to countless victories, securing their place in the world and establishing their dominance through strength and strategy.
- **Thrall**: A visionary orcish leader, Thrall is known for his wisdom, diplomacy, and commitment to orcish freedom. He united the orcish clans, establishing the Horde as a powerful force in the world, and forged alliances with other races, ensuring the survival and prosperity of the orcish people.

This mythology, complete with its pantheon, creation myths, and legendary figures, serves to unify the world's inhabitants, instilling a sense of shared history and cultural identity. It also provides a framework for understanding the world's natural phenomena and societal developments, offering guidance and wisdom to those who seek it.

## Continuity Review
In reviewing the provided history and mythology, I have identified the following areas that require revisions to ensure continuity and consistency:

1. Pantheons:
   - While the pantheons themselves are well-defined and distinct, it is unclear how the different pantheons interact with each other. Consider establishing a framework for inter-pantheon relationships, alliances, and conflicts.
   - The human pantheon lacks a god or goddess associated with craftsmanship or artistry, which is a significant aspect of human culture. Introducing a deity that embodies these domains would improve the cohesion of the human pantheon.

2. Creation Myths:
   - All creation myths share a common theme of gods shaping the world from a void or chaotic state. However, the creation myths differ in their description of the primordial state (Elemental Chaos, Astral Sea, Shadowfell). It would be beneficial to clarify the relationship between these primordial states, such as establishing a shared origin or hierarchy.

3. Legendary Figures:
   - While the legendary figures are well-developed within their respective cultures, there is a lack of interaction between figures from different cultures. Introducing shared experiences, conflicts, or alliances among these figures would help create a more interconnected world.
   - Some cultures, such as the elven and human pantheons, have a greater number of legendary figures compared to others, like the orcish pantheon. Adding more legendary figures to underrepresented cultures would create a more balanced portrayal of each culture's impact on the world.

To address these continuity issues, I recommend the following revisions:

1. Pantheons:
   - Develop a framework for inter-pantheon relationships, outlining alliances, conflicts, and communication channels.
   - Introduce a human deity associated with craftsmanship or artistry, further solidifying the human pantheon.

2. Creation Myths:
   - Clarify the relationship between the primordial states mentioned in each creation myth, such as establishing a shared origin or hierarchy.

3. Legendary Figures:
   - Create shared experiences, conflicts, or alliances among legendary figures from different cultures to promote interconnectivity.
   - Add more legendary figures to underrepresented cultures, ensuring a balanced portrayal of each culture's impact on the world.

By implementing these revisions, you can ensure continuity and consistency in the world's history and mythology, enhancing the overall cohesion and immersion of the narrative.

## Geography and Locations
**Verdantia, the Heart of Aurelia**

Verdantia, the capital city of Aurelia, is a breathtaking urban environment nestled in the heart of a lush, green continent. The city is a testament to the elven love for nature and artistry, boasting an array of elegant buildings adorned with intricate carvings and graceful curves. The architecture is primarily wooden, with buildings blending seamlessly into the surrounding forest, giving the impression that Verdantia is a natural extension of the landscape itself.

Points of interest:
- **The Grand Arbor**: A massive, intricately carved wooden structure that serves as the city's central square and gathering place.
- **Corellon's Sanctuary**: A stunning temple dedicated to the chief elven deity, Corellon Larethian, featuring soaring spires and beautiful gardens.
- **The Erevan Library**: A repository of knowledge and home to many ancient texts, this elegant building is the hub of elven scholarship and learning.

**Frosthold, the Frozen Fortress of Aquilonis**

Frosthold, the dwarven capital of Aquilonis, lies nestled in a valley surrounded by towering peaks. The city is built from the very stone and ice of the mountains themselves, with sturdy stone buildings and icy walkways connecting the various levels. The architecture is functional and strong, reflecting the resilience of the dwarven people who call this harsh continent home.

Points of interest:
- **Moradin's Forge**: A massive, working forge at the heart of the city, where the finest dwarven weapons and armor are crafted.
- **The Frosthold Library**: A treasure trove of dwarven history, lore, and engineering knowledge.
- **The Clangeddin War College**: A prestigious institution dedicated to the training and education of dwarven warriors and soldiers.

**Pyrolyn, the Flame of Ignis**

Pyrolyn is a vibrant and bustling city built around a network of underground oases and caverns, providing respite from the searing heat of the Ignis desert. The city's architecture is a blend of aboveground adobe structures and subterranean chambers, with intricate channels and aqueducts carrying life-giving water throughout the city.

Points of interest:
- **The Grand Oasis**: A massive, lush garden at the heart of the city, providing a haven of greenery and life amidst the arid desert.
- **The Kord Coliseum**: A magnificent arena for various sports and competitions, honoring the human god of storms, battle, and sports.
- **The Sun Temple**: A stunning temple dedicated to Pelor, the human god of the sun, healing, and strength, featuring striking stained-glass windows that capture and diffuse the sunlight.

These three cities showcase the diversity and creativity of the world of Elysium, reflecting the unique cultures, climates, and landscapes of each region.

# Common Character Archetypes of Elysium

This document outlines the common character archetypes found within the different societies and factions of Elysium. These archetypes represent the diverse roles and personalities that shape the world and its cultures.

## 1. The Visionary Leader

- **Description**: Charismatic and inspiring, visionary leaders are skilled at rallying others around a shared goal or ideology. They possess strong strategic minds and are capable of making tough decisions in times of crisis.
- **Example**: Thrall, the visionary orcish warchief, unites disparate orcish clans under a single banner and forges alliances with other races.

## 2. The Protector

- **Description**: Protectors are selfless and dedicated individuals who place the well-being of others above their own. They often serve as guards, bodyguards, or champions, using their skills and abilities to safeguard those in their care.
- **Example**: Galadriel, the ancient elven sorceress, is a skilled diplomat and politician who has devoted her life to protecting elven culture and heritage.

## 3. The Curious Innovator

- **Description**: Curious innovators are driven by their insatiable curiosity and passion for discovery. They excel in various fields, such as art, science, and engineering, and contribute significantly to their societies' progress.
- **Example**: Leonardo da Vinci, the brilliant human artist and inventor, is known for his groundbreaking contributions to science and engineering.

## 4. The Resilient Warrior

- **Description**: Resilient warriors are steadfast and courageous individuals who embody the values of their culture, such as honor, perseverance, and loyalty. They excel in combat and often serve as the defenders of their people.
- **Example**: Thrain, the legendary dwarven king, is a skilled warrior and leader who has led his people to countless victories.

## 5. The Secretive Operative

- **Description**: Secretive operatives are members of clandestine orders and secret societies that work to influence events from the shadows. They are skilled in espionage, sabotage, and other covert operations.
- **Example**: Custodian 13, a former Dean of Infinity, is a member of the Null Consensus and oversees the organization's covert operations.

## 6. The Wandering Wanderer

- **Description**: Wandering Wanderers are nomadic individuals who roam the world in search of adventure, knowledge, or personal growth. They are often skilled in survival, navigation, and making connections with diverse cultures.
- **Example**: A Blind Navigator, a Free League corsair, sails the uncharted seas in search of undiscovered lands and hidden treasures.

## 7. The Guardian of Balance

- **Description**: Guardians of balance work to maintain equilibrium and prevent any single faction from achieving hegemony. They are often subtle in their methods, using diplomacy, misdirection, and manipulation to achieve their goals.
- **Example**: A Gardener, a member of the Veiled Garden, subtly alters probability by introducing seemingly insignificant events that cascade into geopolitical shifts.

These archetypes represent the diverse roles and personalities that shape the world of Elysium. By understanding these archetypes, you can better understand the motivations, strengths, and weaknesses of the characters you encounter in your adventures.
