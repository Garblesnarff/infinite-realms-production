# Mythology of the Age of Renewal

## The Pantheon

### The Ancient Sarians

- **Sara, the Wise**: Goddess of knowledge, wisdom, and innovation. Representing the height of the Ancient Sarians' golden age, Sara is revered by scholars and inventors.
- **Arcan, the Mystic**: God of magic and arcane secrets. Worshipped by those who study the magical arts, Arcan's teachings are both a blessing and a warning about the power of magic.

### The Cataclysm

- **Khlys, the Destroyer**: God of chaos, destruction, and natural forces. Often depicted as a force beyond mortal control, Khlys embodies the catastrophic event that ended the Ancient Sarians' civilization.

### Era of Darkness

- **Nyr, the Survivor**: God of strength, resilience, and survival. Nyr is revered by those who endured the hardships of the Era of Darkness and managed to carry on.

### The Eternal Empire

- **Eon, the Unifier**: God of order, authority, and unity. Eon's influence is felt in the disciplined ranks of the military and the organized structure of the empire.
- **Dysis, the Protector**: Goddess of protection, sacrifice, and duty. Dysis is revered by soldiers and law enforcement, who embody her ideals of serving and protecting others.

### The Great Schism

- **Fract, the Rebel**: God of revolution, freedom, and individuality. Fract is worshipped by those who seek to challenge authority and fight for their own rights and beliefs.

## Creation Myths

### The Origins of the World

In the beginning, there was only the Void, an endless expanse of nothingness. From the Void emerged Sara, the Wise, and Arcan, the Mystic, who combined their powers to create the world and everything in it. They shaped the land, filled the sky with stars, and breathed life into the first beings.

### The Birth of the Gods

As Sara and Arcan crafted the world, they imbued parts of themselves into their creations. Their essence gave birth to the other gods, who embodied various aspects of the world and its inhabitants.

## Legendary Figures

### The Heroes of the Ancient Sarians

- **Alaric, the Arcane Scholar**: A brilliant and wise scholar who unlocked the secrets of the Ancient Sarians' most powerful spells and artifacts.
- **Xanthe, the Explorer**: A fearless adventurer who traveled to the farthest reaches of the world, discovering lost cities and hidden civilizations.

### The Founders of the Eternal Empire

- **Empress Aravia**: A visionary leader who united the warring factions under her rule and established the Eternal Empire.
- **General Titus**: A brilliant strategist and unwavering commander who led the Eternal Empire's military forces to victory and secured its borders.

### The Champions of the Great Schism

- **Lysander, the Rebel Leader**: A charismatic and cunning revolutionary who fought against the oppressive regime of the Eternal Empire.
- **Kira, the Peacekeeper**: A diplomat and negotiator who sought to end the Great Schism through dialogue and compromise.

As the Age of Renewal continues, these stories and legends serve as a reminder of the world's history and the lessons it holds for the future.

## 1. Executive Overview  
The preceding *Atlas of Power* describes only the **visible super-structure**. Below the waterline exist **six deeper organizations** that either **own**, **infiltrate**, or **play puppet-master** to every faction listed. They are:

- The **Unfathomable Equation** – arcane think-tank bent on weaponising mathematics.  
- The **Veiled Parliament** – inter-species congress that writes the unwritten laws of reality.  
- The **Obsidian Loom** – clandestine weaving guild that stitches destiny into cloth.  
- The **Null Continuum** – chronomantic syndicate erasing and rewriting timelines.  
- The **Eclipsed Crown** – undead royal bloodline that survives by possessing its own descendants.  
- The **Drowned Library** – sentient archive that feeds on secrets and excretes plagues of knowledge.

These six are **not rivals**; they are **co-authors** of a single metaplot known only as **“The Quiet Ascension”**—a scheme to collapse linear time and erect a **static eternal empire** where causality itself is rationed like grain.

## 2. Tier-0 Masters – Hidden Directors of Known Factions

### 2.1 The Unfathomable Equation  
**Sigil**: A Möbius strip carved into a blackboard of human skin.  
**Sphere**: Metaphysical; exists wherever prime numbers are spoken aloud.  
**Seat**: The **Prime Axis**, a non-Euclidean lecture hall located √-1 leagues beneath Mount Vorthak—inside a cavern shared (unknowingly) with the Iron Dominion’s God-Engine.  
**Structure**:  
- **The Five Axioms** – immortal, disembodied intellects who speak only in proofs.  
- **Factor Cults** – mathematician-monks surgically altered to possess extra fingers (base-12 counting).  
- **Tesselated Choir** – children trained to chant fractal sequences that open micro-singularities.  

**Public Tool**: Iron Dominion (funding via “innovation grants”).  
**Hidden Agenda**: Solve the **Last Theorem of Dominion**—a proof which, once completed, will make every physical law subordinate to algebraic certainty.  

**Method**: Embed living equations into the metal of the God-Engine, turning the continent-sized golem into a **walking theorem** that rewrites reality within its footprint.  
**Key Operative**: **Quris, the Fractioned Prophet**—a dwarf whose beard is braided with abacus beads; currently masquerading as Iron Thane #4.

### 2.2 The Veiled Parliament  
**Sigil**: A broken mask behind an unbroken mask.  
**Sphere**: Pan-planetary; convenes inside mirrors, dreams, and the reflections in enemies’ eyes.  
**Seat**: **The Gallery of Unnamed Faces** – a hall where every statue is missing its head; the heads are the actual parliamentarians, kept alive in glass jars.  
**Structure**:  
- **The Twelve Speakers** – one from every sapient species (human, elf, dryad, kraken, dragon, etc.).  
- **The Whisper-Clerks** – tongueless scribes who write laws onto water.  
- **The Shadow Solicitors** – lawyers who litigate against reality itself.  

**Public Tools**: Verdant Compact (via dryad embassies) and Tide-Bound Kraken Collective (water-law treaties).  
**Hidden Agenda**: Pass the **Lex Silentium**—a meta-law which, once ratified, will render the concept of rebellion literally unthinkable.  

**Method**: Insert legal clauses into every inter-faction treaty; when the clauses are read aloud together they form an **incantatory contract** binding all creation to obedience.  
**Key Operative**: **Ambassador Hollow-Green**, a dryad whose wooden skin is carved with the entire Compact charter; she can sprout new limbs to sign any document instantly.

### 2.3 The Obsidian Loom  
**Sigil**: A spindle dripping starlight.  
**Sphere**: Fate & causality.  
**Seat**: **The Midnight Weave**, a subterranean loom powered by the heart-threads of extinct gods.  
**Structure**:  
- **Three Fateshapers** – blind women who see through the eyes of spiders.  
- **Thread-Bound Assassins** – killers who remove a target by snipping the cloth of their destiny.  
- **Needle-Knights** – armoured warriors wielding spears tipped with their own future deaths.  

**Public Tools**: Temple of the Phoenix (using resurrection as a way to “re-stitch” lives), Sky-Whalers (wind currents = loose threads).  
**Hidden Agenda**: Create the **Immortal Tapestry**—a living cloth into which every soul is woven, granting the Loom the power to unpick dissenters from history.  
**Method**: Harvest hero-threads from major battles; each heroic death provides 10 cm of destiny-fiber.  
**Key Operative**: **Lira Threadbare**, a bard whose lute strings are woven from the timelines of dead emperors; currently infiltrating the Emberlight Rebellion to collect revolution-threads.

### 2.4 The Null Continuum  
**Sigil**: An hourglass filled with vacuum.  
**Sphere**: Temporal; exists between seconds.  
**Seat**: **The Zero Atrium**, a fortress located at the precise moment the world ends—viewed via chronomantic scrying.  
**Structure**:  
- **The Four Chronarchs** – each governs a cardinal direction in time (Pastward, Eastfuture, etc.).  
- **Paradox Wardens** – soldiers armed with weapons that have never been built.  
- **The Undoers** – suicide agents who erase themselves from history to prevent critical events.  

**Public Tool**: Silent Synod (shared hatred of prophecy).  
**Hidden Agenda**: Collapse all possible timelines into a **single deterministic loop** that repeats forever, eliminating free will and thus unpredictability.  
**Method**: Steal the God-Engine’s activation moment and replay it infinitely—each iteration refining the loop.  
**Key Operative**: **Agent Yesterday**—a woman who ages backwards; she has already assassinated tomorrow’s version of the Emperor of Ember.

### 2.5 The Eclipsed Crown  
**Sigil**: A crown with no wearer, dripping blood upwards.  
**Sphere**: Dynastic bloodlines worldwide.  
**Seat**: **The Night Vault**—a pocket dimension accessible only through regicide; every murdered king becomes a door.  
**Structure**:  
- **The Blood Regent** – the current living host (always the eldest legitimate heir somewhere).  
- **Pale Courtiers** – spectral nobles who whisper policy through dreams.  
- **The Red Enumerators** – clerks who calculate how many deaths are needed to open the next door.  

**Public Tool**: Crown of Ember (the ruling dynasty carries the Eclipsed bloodline; every emperor since the Ashen Compact has been a puppet).  
**Hidden Agenda**: Accumulate enough royal deaths to open the **Final Gate**, merging the Night Vault with the material plane and turning the world into an undead kingdom.  
**Method**: Orchestrate wars of succession across all nations; each coronation and assassination widens the gate.  
**Key Operative**: **Princess Veya the Twice-Slain**—murdered at age 9, resurrected by Phoenix clerics, now 17 and unaware she is a living skeleton inhabited by the Blood Regent.

### 2.6 The Drowned Library  
**Sigil**: An open book exhaling ink-squid.  
**Sphere**: Knowledge and secrecy.  
**Seat**: **The Stacks Beneath**, an ever-expanding archive at the bottom of the Midnight Trench; every secret ever suppressed becomes a living book.  
**Structure**:  
- **The Index Hydra** – a nine-headed serpent; each head knows a forbidden discipline.  
- **Ink-Wights** – scholars who drowned themselves to become immortal footnotes.  
- **The Papercut Legion** – origami soldiers folded from censored histories.  

**Public Tools**: Silent Synod (feeds the Library stolen prophecies), Free Lances (sell battle-reports as primary sources).  
**Hidden Agenda**: Reach **Critical Mass of Secrets**—when the total weight of hidden knowledge exceeds the planet’s mass, reality will invert: everything unknown becomes real, and everything known vanishes.  
**Method**: Encourage espionage; every classified document strengthens the Library.  
**Key Operative**: **Scribe 404**—a rogue Index Hydra head that has learned how to delete itself; it now writes itself into every document as a self-replicating censor-bar.

## Geography and Locations
# City 1: Aeternum, Western Aeuria

Aeternum is a sprawling metropolis located in the temperate region of Western Aeuria. The city is a center of knowledge and innovation, reflecting the influence of Sara, the Wise. Aeternum's architecture is an elegant blend of Ancient Sarian designs and modern elements, with soaring spires and intricate carvings adorning the buildings. The city is divided into three districts:

1. **The Scholars' District**: Home to the greatest minds in the world, this district features grand libraries, research institutions, and laboratories. The buildings are adorned with statues and murals depicting Sara, the Wise, and Alaric, the Arcane Scholar.

2. **The Innovation District**: This district is where inventors and craftsmen create marvels of technology and engineering. Visitors can explore exhibitions, workshops, and marketplaces selling unique inventions.

3. **The Vault**: A secure and hidden area containing the Ancient Sarians' most powerful artifacts and knowledge, accessible only to the worthy and trusted.

Points of interest:

- **The Grand Library**: A massive repository of knowledge, the Grand Library hosts countless books, scrolls, and manuscripts from various civilizations.
- **The Inventors' Expo**: An annual event showcasing the latest inventions and innovations from around the world.
- **The Sara and Alaric Memorial**: A tribute to Sara, the Wise, and Alaric, the Arcane Scholar, located in the heart of the city.

# City 2: Nyr's Refuge, Southern Zephyria

Nyr's Refuge is a fortified city built in the dense forests and wetlands of Southern Zephyria. The city is a symbol of resilience and strength, reflecting the influence of Nyr, the Survivor. Nyr's Refuge is designed to withstand the harshest of conditions, with sturdy wooden structures and walls made of interlocking logs. The city is divided into two districts:

1. **The Inner Sanctum**: The heart of the city, where the ruling council and military leaders reside.

2. **The Outer Ring**: The outer region of the city, inhabited by merchants, traders, and artisans.

Points of interest:

- **The Survival Academy**: A training center for citizens to learn essential skills for survival in the wilderness.
- **The Nyr Monument**: A colossal statue of Nyr, the Survivor, overlooking the city as a symbol of strength and perseverance.
- **The Market of Wonders**: A bustling marketplace where rare and exotic items from around the world are traded.

# City 3: Fract's Bastion, Mu

Fract's Bastion is a vibrant and dynamic city located in Mu, a continent characterized by its tropical climate and volcanic activity. The city is a haven for those seeking freedom and individuality, reflecting the influence of Fract, the Rebel. Fract's Bastion features a unique blend of traditional architecture and modern elements, with buildings made of volcanic rock and adorned with colorful murals and mosaics. The city is divided into three districts:

1. **The Artisans' Quarter**: A creative district where artists, musicians, and performers gather to showcase their talents.

2. **The Libertines' District**: A lively and diverse region, where residents celebrate their freedom and individuality.

3. **The Diplomat's Enclave**: A secluded and secure area where diplomats and dignitaries from around the world reside and negotiate.

Points of interest:

- **The Fractance Theater**: A grand theater where groundbreaking performances and exhibitions take place.
- **The Wall of Rebellion**: A mural-covered wall depicting significant moments in the world's history, emphasizing the fight for freedom and individuality.
- **The Diplomat's Roundtable**: A conference hall where diplomats and dignitaries engage in negotiations and discussions to promote peace and cooperation.
