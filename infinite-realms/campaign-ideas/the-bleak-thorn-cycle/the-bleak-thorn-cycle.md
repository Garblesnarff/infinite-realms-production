# The Bleak Thorn Cycle

*A Dark-Fantasy / Gritty campaign — Short Campaign (6–8 sessions) — Medium difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** The Bleak Thorn Cycle
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Dark Fantasy (heavy horror flavor, twisted folklore)
**Estimated Length:** Short Campaign — 6–8 sessions (each session ≈ 3–4 hours)
**Player Level Range:** Start **3** → Finish **7–8** (adjust encounters upward if players are stronger)
**Tone (keywords):** grim, melancholic, suspenseful, morally grey, creeping dread
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
An ancient hedge of black brambles — the Bleak Thorn — has begun to reclaim the borderlands, bringing nightmares, twisted fauna, and the slow rot of memory. The party, once ordinary folk touched by loss or debt, discover the hedge grows from a sleeping god’s wound and a small cult's bargaining. They must choose whether to heal, remove, or weaponize the thorn — and each choice will exact a cost.

---

## What “Medium” Difficulty Means Here

* **Encounters:** Mix of medium-to-hard CR encounters (party of 4–5): 2–3 combats per session; at least one combat that can be solved diplomatically or stealthily.
* **Puzzles/Survival:** Environmental hazards (poisonous pollen, fog that obscures time), one multi-step puzzle per campaign arc.
* **Consequences:** Failure rarely means TPK, but it costs resources, NPCs, or increases the Campaign Corruption (sanity) meter.

---

## Story Arc (Short Campaign — 6–8 sessions)

A three-act arc with rising stakes, each act \~2–3 sessions.

* **Act I — The Encroaching Hedge (Sessions 1–2):** Mystery & investigation. The hedge grows; villagers forget things; animals behave strangely. The party learns of the Bleak Thorn and a missing druid.
* **Act II — The Cult & the Wound (Sessions 3–5):** Confrontations with cultists who tend the thorn, expeditions into thorn-corrupted places, and the reveal: the thorn grows from the wound of a sleeping nature-god bound long ago. The party must decide whether to find a ritual to close the wound, cut the thorn physically, or harness it.
* **Act III — Choice & Consequence (Sessions 6–8):** Final confrontation at the Wound: ritual, siege, or moral reckoning. Multiple endings depending on the party’s choices (purge, seal, or control).

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Session 1 — “Where Memory Fades”

* **Focus:** Investigation / Social / Low combat
* **Key Events:**

  * Hook: The party helps at a market where a child forgets their mother; rumor of missing shepherds.
  * Explore: Village of Harrowfield; NPCs reveal hedge sightings.
  * Encounter: Thorn-warped wolves (2 shadows + 2 wolves or 4 wolves with poisoned bite; CR \~2 combined).
  * Clue: A pale oak with thorn vines that saps memory — a scrap of cult cloth.

**Escalation:** Players realize this is region-wide and not a local phenomenon.

---

### Session 2 — “Roots in the Marsh”

* **Focus:** Exploration / Hazard / Puzzle
* **Key Events:**

  * Travel to the Marsh of Hollowmist where the hedge meets bog.
  * Environmental hazard: pollen fog causes short hallucinations (weakness on Wisdom saves).
  * Puzzle: An ancient standing stone requires arranging carved leaves (riddle + skill checks).
  * Combat: Thorn-tendriled oozes or blights (one medium CR fight).
  * Discovery: A wounded druid, Eirlys, hiding and muttering about “the wound beneath the hill.”

**Escalation:** Eirlys hints the thorn is fed by a “wound” under the hill of Aderon.

---

### Session 3 — “The Cult of the Slow Sap”

* **Focus:** Social / Stealth / Combat options
* **Key Events:**

  * Infiltrate or parley with a cult outpost in the ruined watchtower.
  * Roleplay: Cultist leader Lysandre argues the thorn brings “remembrance” by taking pain.
  * Combat: Cultists + thorn familiars (or a fight can be avoided with proper diplomacy).
  * Loot/Intel: A torn page of a binding ritual and a map to the Wound.

**Escalation:** The party learns the thorn is a consequence of a ritual gone wrong centuries ago.

---

### Session 4 — “Under the Bone Hill”

* **Focus:** Dungeon crawl / Investigation / Moral choice
* **Key Events:**

  * Delve into crypts below Bone Hill where the god’s shrine was sealed.
  * Encounters: Animated urns, thorn-spawned skeletal guardians, traps (poisonous spores).
  * Puzzle/Mechanic: Corruption checks every time players interact with the central shrine (see mechanics).
  * Reveal: The god’s wound is a petrified heart — infections of grief and bargain.

**Escalation:** Party gains three options: perform a *Healing Sealing* ritual (hard, requires rare components), physically prune the thorn (dangerous but direct), or bargain with Cult’s higher priest to use thorn power.

---

### Session 5 — “Crossroads of Blood and Petal”

* **Focus:** Social / Large skirmish / Resource management
* **Key Events:**

  * The party must gather components: a midnight bloom, a well-curing herb, and a voice from a willow (each a short side-quest or skill challenge).
  * Meanwhile, rival parties/cult escalation — cult marshals an assault on Harrowfield to protect the thorn.
  * Combat: Defend village (mini-battle with waves + hazards) — appropriate CR scaling.
  * Consequence: If the village falls, its people are assimilated into the hedge (adds Corruption).

**Escalation:** Time pressure — ritual must be done at the Wound during the blood-moon.

---

### Session 6 — “The Wound” (Climax)

* **Focus:** Big set-piece / Multi-layered encounter / Moral verdict
* **Key Events:**

  * Final approach through thorn-scarred lands; navigation and final traps.
  * Large combat or dramatic ritual scene (depending on choice): either boss fight (Cult Matriarch + Thorn Avatar / corrupted nature-god projector) or a ritual with skill checks contested by cultists.
  * Multiple endings: Seal the wound (cost: one PC permanently affected), Cut the thorn (big destruction, collateral damage), or Bargain (gain a thorn boon with long-term consequences).

**Escalation / Resolution:** Epilogues for the region based on outcome and Corruption level.

*(If campaign runs 7–8 sessions: add Session 7 for fallout & epilogue, Session 8 for a hidden twist — the wound was only a valve; something larger stirs.)*

## Major NPCs (5–8)

1. **Eirlys Thorne** — *Wounded Druid*

   * **Role:** Initial ally; knows old rites.
   * **Personality:** World-weary, soft-spoken, obsessive over plants.
   * **Secret/Flaw:** She once performed a mercy-binding that birthed thorn tendrils; guilt-driven; possible corruption risk (may betray to save “nature”).

2. **Lysandre Vael** — *Cult Outpost Leader*

   * **Role:** Antagonist with philosophical arguments.
   * **Personality:** Charismatic, earnest, believes pain is the root of memory.
   * **Secret/Flaw:** He manipulates villagers for love of his lost child, whose memory is kept in the thorn.

3. **Matron Halan** — *Village Elder of Harrowfield*

   * **Role:** Moral compass / quest-giver.
   * **Personality:** Iron-willed, pragmatic, anxious about losing grandchildren.
   * **Secret/Flaw:** She once agreed to the thorn bargains decades ago (guilt). Will be emotionally devastated if village is harmed.

4. **Brother Cass** — *Wandering Cleric / Antagonist-ally*

   * **Role:** Offers divine perspective; can aid ritual or oppose it.
   * **Personality:** Righteous, inflexible; haunted by visions.
   * **Secret/Flaw:** His deity whispers solutions that may demand sacrifices.

5. **The Matriarch of Slow Sap (Ilya the Bender)** — *Cult Matriarch*

   * **Role:** Final antagonist if combat chosen.
   * **Personality:** Calm, patient, monstrous devotion.
   * **Secret/Flaw:** Seeks to become one with the thorn — can be reasoned with (or corrupted further).

6. **Merrin of the Sundered Bell** — *Rival treasure hunter / opportunist*

   * **Role:** Optional ally/foil who wants to harvest thorn power for coin.
   * **Personality:** Charming, opportunistic.
   * **Secret/Flaw:** He will betray if it profits him.

1. **Harrowfield** — a small border village with hollowed memory wells and a market of faded goods. (Social hub, moral anchor)
2. **Marsh of Hollowmist** — misty wetlands where time blurs and pollen fog causes hallucinations. (Exploration + hazard)
3. **Bone Hill / The Old Shrine** — subterranean crypt with the petrified heart (dungeon crawl). (Investigation + boss lead-in)
4. **The Ruined Watchtower** — cult outpost; contains records and ritual fragments. (Stealth/social)
5. **The Bleak Thorn Proper** — black bramble fields, animated flora, and ruined homesteads. (Final approach + environmental danger)
6. **The Withered Grove** — ancient willow that bargains in memories — source of one ritual component. (Mystic side-quest).

## Central Conflict

**Main Tension:** A binding wound beneath the borderland feeds a hedge that consumes memory, identity, and life. The conflict is between preservation (stop the spread at cost), exploitation (use thorn power), and appeasement (the cult’s logic). It’s both physical (stop the spread) and philosophical (what is memory worth?).

## Unique Mechanics (Optional, fit Dark Fantasy + Gritty + Medium)

### 1. **Corruption (or Bleakness) Track**

* **Scale:** 0 → 10. Starts 0. Each time players interact with the thorn in certain ways (drinking sap, failing a ritual, letting villagers be assimilated) add 1–3 points.
* **Effects:**

  * 1–3: minor whispers — disadvantage on one social check once per session.
  * 4–6: nightmares — vulnerability to psychic damage during first combat each day; small compulsions.
  * 7–9: visible mutation (pale streak, thorn-like scars); advantage on Intimidation but disadvantage on Persuasion.
  * 10: permanent cost — one character gains a boon (thorn craft) but loses a major humanity trait (no longer able to benefit from long-term rest fully until redeemed).

**Redemption:** Corruption can be reduced by performing rites (costly components), sacrificing rare resource, or a major act of compassion (saving a mass of villagers at risk). Reducing 1–2 per big effort.

### 2. **Memory Checks**

* Wisdom saving throws when exposed to strong thorn magic. Failures cost short-term memory (forget spells prepared, or lose last long rest benefits until remedied).

### 3. **Ritual Skill Challenge (The Sealing)**

* During Act III, the sealing ritual is a contested skill challenge: players contribute different skills (Nature, Religion, Performance, Arcana, Medicine). Cultists attempt to interrupt. Each success brings the ritual closer; each failure adds Corruption or spawns enemies.

### 4. **Optional: Thorn Boons & Curses**

* If party bargains with the thorn, grant one mechanical boon (e.g., +1 to HP regen, thorn-summoning once per day) balanced with a curse (occasional disadvantage on empathy/social checks; attract predators).

## Rewards & Magic Items (Examples)

* **Sap of Remembering (Wondrous, rare):** Apply to an object to reveal its last owner's key memory (once). Causes Corruption +1 on use.
* **Thorn-etched Locket (Uncommon):** Once per long rest, re-roll one failed Wisdom save. If used after Corruption ≥5, it numbs a memory permanently.
* **Willow’s Whisper (Consumable):** Grants advantage on one Charisma (Persuasion) check with nature spirits, but gives a haunting whisper for 24 hours.

## DM Tips & Prep (Minimal prep for immediate play)

* **Prep 15–30 minutes before each session:** Decide which side-quests are active; print 3 key NPC notes; pick 2 combat maps (village + shrine).
* **Use handouts:** torn ritual pages, a map fragment, and a villager’s letter that fades with lemon juice (fun prop).
* **Pacing:** Keep investigation scenes short (20–30 minutes), then alternate 30–60 minute combat/exploration scenes.
* **Moral Beats:** Force choice moments with real consequences — e.g., saving a child means failing to gather a ritual herb.
* **Scaling:** If party is larger/stronger, increase number of minions rather than single higher-CR foes to maintain grit.

---

## Multiple Endings (Examples)

1. **Seal the Wound (Sacrifice):** The party seals the wound fully; the hedge recedes; cost: permanent loss (one PC or NPC memory) — village saved but at emotional cost.
2. **Cut the Thorn (Violent Purge):** Hedge is burned away causing ecological shock — immediate safety, long-term blight on lands and surviving wildlife.
3. **Embrace/Control (Bargain):** The party turns the hedge into a tool — regional power for them, but corruption spreads slowly; the campaign gains darker future hooks.
4. **Failed/Compromised:** Cult succeeds partially — the hedge becomes a living necropolis; set up a sequel.
