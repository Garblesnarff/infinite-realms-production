# Gears & Grimoires: The Cogwheel Conspiracy

*A Steampunk Magitech Campaign — Medium Campaign (9-11 sessions) — Medium difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** Gears & Grimoires: The Cogwheel Conspiracy
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Steampunk Magitech / Industrial Fantasy / Conspiracy
**Estimated Length:** Medium Campaign — 9-11 sessions
**Player Level Range:** Start **2** → Finish **8**
**Tone (keywords):** innovative, industrial, discovery-focused, tense, revolutionary
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
In the sprawling industrial city of Cogwheel Harbor, magic and machinery exist in an uneasy alliance, where enchanted automatons labor alongside human workers and spell-powered factories churn out wonders of magitech engineering. When a series of "accidents" begins befalling prominent citizens, the players discover a conspiracy to replace free will with mechanical obedience through a dangerous new form of mind-controlling magitech. As investigators, whistleblowers, or concerned citizens, they must navigate the city's labyrinthine politics, corporate espionage, and revolutionary undercurrents to expose the conspiracy. But as they delve deeper into the mystery, they discover that the real threat isn't just control—it's the fundamental question of what it means to be human in a world where magic and machines are becoming indistinguishable.

---

## Story Arc (Medium Campaign — 9-11 sessions)

The fusion of progress and peril.

*   **Act I — The Broken Machine (Sessions 1–3):** The players experience a magitech accident that draws them into the investigation. They discover the first hints of corporate conspiracy and meet key allies in the industrial underworld.
*   **Act II — The Assembly Line of Secrets (Sessions 4–7):** As the players infiltrate factories and research facilities, they uncover the conspiracy's scope. They must navigate worker revolts, corporate espionage, and their own moral dilemmas about progress.
*   **Act III — The Final Upgrade (Sessions 8–11):** The players confront the conspiracy's mastermind in a climactic battle that forces them to choose between destroying the dangerous magitech or harnessing its power for good.

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Session 1 — "The Malfunctioning Heart"
*   **Focus:** Introduction / Investigation
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The players witness or experience a magitech accident involving a prominent citizen's mechanical heart replacement.
    *   Initial investigation reveals the "accident" was sabotage, with magical signatures that shouldn't exist in pure machinery.
    *   They meet their first contact in the industrial district - a grizzled mechanic who suspects foul play.
**Escalation:** A corporate enforcer approaches the players, offering "protection" while subtly threatening them to drop the investigation.

### Sessions 2-3 — "The Factory Floor"
*   **Focus:** Infiltration / Social Dynamics
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The players infiltrate the massive factory complex where the accident occurred, discovering evidence of experimental magitech.
    *   They navigate complex worker relationships, from exploited laborers to ambitious overseers.
    *   A breakthrough clue leads them to a hidden research facility beneath the factory.
**Escalation:** The conspiracy activates basic security measures, forcing the players into a desperate escape while factory automatons come alive with unnatural purpose.

### Sessions 4-5 — "The Corporate Ladder"
*   **Focus:** Politics / Espionage
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The players attend a high-society event at the corporate headquarters, gathering intelligence from elite guests.
    *   They discover evidence of mind-control magitech being tested on willing and unwilling subjects.
    *   A key witness provides them with a data crystal containing classified research files.
**Escalation:** The conspiracy identifies the players as a threat, activating corporate spies and automated hunters to eliminate them.

### Sessions 6-7 — "The Underground Network"
*   **Focus:** Alliance Building / Combat
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The players connect with the revolutionary underground, a network of workers fighting against magitech exploitation.
    *   They participate in a raid on a conspiracy outpost, gathering crucial evidence and allies.
    *   A moral dilemma arises when they discover the conspiracy's magitech could help the disabled but at the cost of free will.
**Escalation:** The conspiracy unleashes prototype combat automatons, forcing the players into intense battles that blend magical and mechanical combat.

### Sessions 8-9 — "The Heart of Innovation"
*   **Focus:** Deep Infiltration / Revelation
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The players infiltrate the conspiracy's central research facility, the Heart of Innovation.
    *   They confront the lead researcher, learning the full scope of the mind-control conspiracy.
    *   A critical choice: Destroy the facility immediately or gather more evidence at greater risk.
**Escalation:** The facility's defense systems activate, creating a multi-phase battle against increasingly sophisticated magitech guardians.

### Sessions 10-11 — "The Choice of Progress"
*   **Focus:** Climax / Resolution
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The players confront the conspiracy's mastermind in a final showdown.
    *   They must decide the fate of the dangerous magitech - complete destruction, controlled development, or revolutionary redistribution.
    *   The campaign concludes with the city's transformation, for better or worse.
**Escalation:** The mastermind activates a city-wide magitech network, forcing players to fight on multiple fronts while making impossible choices.

## Major NPCs

1.  **Professor Elara Voss** — *The Visionary Researcher*
    *   **Role:** The lead researcher of the conspiracy, a brilliant mind who believes the ends justify the means in the name of progress.
    *   **Personality:** Intellectual and passionate, with a cold rationality that borders on fanaticism. She sees herself as humanity's savior.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** She was once crippled by a magitech accident and now uses experimental implants, making her both brilliant and increasingly unstable.

2.  **Marcus "Sparks" Rodriguez** — *The Revolutionary Mechanic*
    *   **Role:** A former factory worker turned revolutionary leader who becomes the players' guide to the industrial underworld.
    *   **Personality:** Charismatic and street-smart, with a dry wit and deep-seated anger at corporate exploitation.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** He has a brother who was "converted" by the conspiracy's magitech, creating a personal stake in the conflict.

3.  **Lady Cassandra Vale** — *The Corporate Executive*
    *   **Role:** A high-ranking executive in the conspiracy who begins to question the morality of their work.
    *   **Personality:** Ambitious and sophisticated, maintaining a facade of corporate professionalism while hiding growing doubts.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** She knows the players from her past and may become an ally if they can appeal to her conscience rather than her ambition.

## Key Locations

1.  **The Grand Factory Complex:** A massive industrial facility where magic and machinery produce wonders and horrors in equal measure.
2.  **The Heart of Innovation:** A secret research facility hidden beneath the city where the conspiracy develops their mind-control magitech.
3.  **The Corporate Spire:** A towering skyscraper of steel and spellwork that serves as the conspiracy's headquarters.
4.  **The Undercity Network:** A labyrinth of maintenance tunnels and worker hideouts beneath the city where revolutionaries plot.

---

## Central Conflict

This campaign explores the dark side of progress, the ethics of human augmentation, and the struggle for workers' rights in an industrial-magitech society. The central conflict revolves around a conspiracy to use magitech mind control to create a "perfect" society, forcing players to confront questions about free will, progress, and humanity. The players must navigate corporate politics, worker revolutions, and their own moral compasses while deciding whether to destroy dangerous technology or harness it for good.

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Magitech Modification System**
*   Players can acquire and modify magitech devices, from simple tools to complex implants.
*   Each modification provides benefits but may have side effects or moral consequences.
*   Players must balance the power of magitech augmentation against the risk of losing their humanity.

### 2. **The Conspiracy Web**
*   A network of clues and connections that players map out as they investigate.
*   Connecting clues reveals new information and opportunities, but also alerts the conspiracy to the players' progress.
*   The web evolves based on player choices, creating branching paths and multiple endings.

### 3. **Industrial Hazard System**
*   The factory environment creates ongoing hazards that require creative problem-solving.
*   Steam vents, automated machinery, and magical radiation create dynamic challenges.
*   Players must use both technical skills and magical knowledge to navigate these dangers.
